## The one idea: pace to 26 hours, not 24

Beating these ETAs should feel like **banking confidence**, not falling behind. If
he's arriving *before* the times below, he is on track for a sub-26 finish and
everything is working. We do **not** chase 24 and we do **not** panic if a stop runs
a little long. The governor is the realistic target; staying calm and cool *is* the
race plan.

## Master prep (do this before the race)

### Cooler & ice plan — the most important crew job
- Big cooler at **every** crew stop: ice + pre-filled water bottles, kept cold.
- A **dedicated ice bag** at each stop for refilling the **ice bandana** and the hat.
- **Insulated soft flasks** so the water he carries stays cold between stops.
- Rule: **he never leaves a crew stop without two cold bottles and a fresh ice bandana.**

### What EVERY crew stop needs ready (pre-stage before he arrives)
- [ ] 2 cold bottles — **1 carb mix + 1 plain cold water**
- [ ] Fresh **ice bandana** + a ziploc of ice to carry out
- [ ] Gels counted for the next leg **+1 spare** (see each stop below)
- [ ] **Flash IV** / salt caps
- [ ] **Lube** (Squirrel's Nut Butter / BodyGlide) for reapplication
- [ ] Dry / night kit **if** the next leg runs past sunset (~8:50 PM)

### The 4 checks (ask at every stop)
1. **Stomach** — eating OK? nausea? → if off, slow down, switch to savory/broth.
2. **Feet / chafing** — any hotspots? → fix *now* with lube/leukotape, don't wait.
3. **Mood** — dark patch? → calories + cold water + encouragement.
4. **Urine color** — clear-pale = good; dark = drink; very clear + lots = ease fluids, add sodium.

### Drop bags (at Oak Hill, Silver Springs, Finish)
Spare socks ×2 · spare shorts + shirt · lube stick · blister/foot kit · gel resupply ·
salt caps. **Night bags** (reached after dark) add: headlamp + batteries, warm dry layer.

### Night kit — hand it off at **Silver Springs (mile 50.7)**
Sunset is ~8:50 PM. The next crew stop after Silver Springs (Pine Hollow, mile 67.2)
isn't until ~9:09 PM — he'll hit the dark mid-leg around mile 60. So the **headlamp,
backup light, reflective vest, and a dry warm shirt go out from Silver Springs**, even
though it's still light there. Silver Springs is also the **pacer pickup** (mile 50.7).

## Cooling playbook (escalate all of this in heat, >75°F)

This is the heart of his race. Run through the relevant items at every stop:

- **Ice bandana** — ice in a buff/bandana over the neck (cools the carotids). **Refresh every stop.**
- **One bottle always cold water** — straight from the cooler.
- **Ice in the hat / under the cap**; wet the cap.
- **Ice in arm / cooling sleeves** against the forearms.
- **Ice in the hands** to hold and let melt — palmar cooling is very effective.
- **Douse** head / neck / back with cold water at every aid and creek crossing.
- **Cooling towel** (PVA chamois) around the neck.
- Ice to **wrists, neck, armpits, groin** — the major vessels.
- Light, loose, **white** wicking clothing; **wettable sun sleeves**.
- **Walk the climbs**, ease the pace through the **10 AM – 4 PM heat window**.
- **Between crew stops he can top up ice at:** Schumacher, Botzum, Robinson Field,
  Indigo Lake (self-serve jugs).

> ⚠️ **Cooling ↔ chafing tradeoff:** dousing wets the skin and raises chafe risk.
> **Reapply lube after he's been doused.**

## Chafing playbook

- **Pre-race:** lube all hotspots (thighs/groin, underarms, **tape nipples**, waistband, between toes).
- Seamless, well-fitted, wicking apparel — **no cotton**.
- **Crew reapplies lube at every stop** — more critical once he's wet from sweat/dousing/rain.
- **Sock + foot-powder change** at drop-bag stops.
- Fix hotspots **before they blister** — leukotape on first contact.
- Blister kit: leukotape, needle, alcohol wipes, foot powder, spare socks.

## Fueling target

**~60 g carb/hr** (≈ 2.5 gels/hr) · **~500–700 mg sodium/hr** (700+ in heat) via
**Flash IV** + salt caps · **20–24 oz fluid/hr**. Hand the exact gel count per leg
(below) **+1 spare**. Keep the carb mix cold too. After ~12 h / dark, shift to **savory**
(broth, ramen, potatoes — the return aid stations stock all of it). Eat before hungry.

## Per-stop decision tree (governor logic)

At each stop, compare his arrival to the **26h ETA**:
- **In before the ETA →** on plan. Quick, efficient stop. Stay disciplined — **don't let him chase 24.**
- **Right at the ETA →** fine. Run the full checklist, keep him cool and fueled.
- **Behind the ETA →** diagnose the cause and fix it, don't just push pace:
  - Hot / overheating → **escalate cooling** (ice everywhere, cold water, slow through heat).
  - Stomach off → slow down, **savory/broth**, settle the gut.
  - Feet/chafing → **fix it now**, it compounds.
  - Big cushion to cutoff at every stop (~3+ hours) → **there is time to fix problems. Calm.**
